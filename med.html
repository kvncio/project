Medina

Medina is called by Muslims “The Good Way,” 
the first capital in the history of Islam , and the second holiest place for Muslims after Mecca.
In the west of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, Medina is about 400 km away from Mecca in the northeastern direction, and about 150 km east of the Red Sea, and its closest port is the port of Yanbu, which is located on the western-southern side of it and is 220 km away from it.
